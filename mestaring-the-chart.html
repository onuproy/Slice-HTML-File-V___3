Have you ever wondered what trading charts and psychologists have in common?

Probably not, but as it turns out...

Both are really good at identifying human emotions.

These behaviors repeat time and time again - optimism creates demand, and fear creates supply. There is constant tug-of-war between the two sides and the chart is there to show you who is winning the battle.

The two most popular charts are the candlestick chart and the line chart. You can use both but each really shines for a particular approach to trading.

Line charts typically connect historical closing prices and reduce noise from movements that are not relevant to long term investors. It allows you to quickly gauge the trend, who‚Äôs in control of the price and for how long.

Best for Traders - Candlestick chart

This chart offers 5x the information compared to a line chart. It comes it handy when scouting for patterns and short term price moves.

Notice how the opening and closing prices are reversed - bearish candles open higher, the price falls and closes lower. Bullish candles open lower, the price rises and closes higher.

Each individual candle is a story on its own, providing valuable data on the battle between buyers and sellers.

If you love the adrenaline rush of trading actively - you will buy, short, use leverage and have a blast with the candlestick chart.

For a more laid-back approach - use the line chart to look for opportunities to buy and invest at a discount.
