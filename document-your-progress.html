Trading Psychology • Lesson 3

Document Your Progress

5 min read

A trading journal is one of the best ways to keep bad habits at bay. It can be an app on your phone, an excel file on your computer, or the old-fashioned way; a simple pen and paper notebook. It is up to you how you organize your trading journal, but here are a few pointers for a start:

  • Entry and exit date.
  • Trade size and % of total capital.
  • Stop-loss and take-profit levels.
  • Profits or losses from the trade.
  • Your mood on the day of the trade.
  • What could you do better next time.

Over 83% of successful traders and investors keep a log of every trade they make to track their progress over time - you should too!
