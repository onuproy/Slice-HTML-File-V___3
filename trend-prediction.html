Trend Prediction with News

3 min read

Both good news and bad news can have a meaningful impact on the price of an asset, and much like fish, they should be consumed when fresh.

Uncertainty and negative news usually attracts selling pressure. If the scales tip and supply exceeds demand the price will fall.

When news are hopeful and optimistic, traders expect an increase in demand and often expectation alone causes prices to rise.

Explained: Uncertainty

Economies and investments are based on future projections. You invest in a stock because you are certain that its future looks bright and the price will rise.

While there is always some level of uncertainty about the future, when something shocking and unexpected is published in the news, it creates lots of uncertainty ,making future predictions becomes much harder.

This explains how sometimes negative news, if they are expected, can affect the price positively, because the markets have prepared for them.
