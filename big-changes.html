Explained: What is inflation?

Inflation is the gradual rise in prices caused by an increasing amount of money in the economy. Governments print money to stimulate the economy, but this has to be balanced with equal economic activity to keep inflation targets healthy ~ 2%.

At this rate, it enables economic growth. As a result, wages and prices can adjust accordingly, and the impact of inflation is invisible in everyday life.

When inflation rises unexpectedly, it leads to uncertainty about the economy. The value of savings and investment returns decreases and if wages can't keep up - people get less goods for the same money.
