3 Steps To Your First Trade

5 min read

Let’s quickly walk through your first trade. If you feel ready to trade right away, this will help. You will study in-depth tactics and nuances for greater results later in the course

Step 1 - What to trade?

The best known asset you have the most information on

Step 2 - trade size?

For beginner traders we recommend not more than 5% of your capital

Step 3 is determining the use of leverage.

Leverage is a loan from the broker that let’s you trade with more money than you currently have.

The example 👇 shows how even a modest trade that's leveraged and executed well can quickly result in big profits.

How does leverage work?

Let's say you want to trade with $200 at 1x leverage (no leverage).

For every 1% move in the market, you can gain or lose $2 (1% of $200).

Now, if you were to trade with the same $200, this time at 10x leverage, your position size would be $2,000(200x10).

Now, for every 1% move, you can gain or lose $20 (1% of $2000).

This can be beneficial for growing a small trading account quickly but high leverage also carries more risk as it multiplies your losses if the trade goes against you - Your risk management and mental state must be rock solid! (more on this later😉)

Step 4 setting up stop loss and take profit.

Basicaly it’s a stress remover of having to constantly monitor your trades.

SL will close the trade automatically if the market moves against you while TP closes the trade and locks in your profit when it’s succesful.

Succesful traders combine these tools to achieve a 3:1 Risk/Reward ratio

You buy a stock at $100 expecting it will rise to $130 in 5 days.

You set your TP💰 at $130 and your SL⛔️ at $90 just in case

You’re risking $10 for a chance to earn 3x more - $30

In a setup like this only 25+1% of your trades need to be winners for you be succesful in the long run.

You should not be right every time. Instead be consistent and always win a bit more than you lose.
