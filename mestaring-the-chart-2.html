There is an old saying among Wall-Street veterans - “the trend is your friend”, so you should never trade against it.

So in a downtrend like this it would be best short-sell right?

Well you’d be wrong...

Switching to a bigger timeframe shows us that this is just a minor correction in a strong long-term uptrend - a great buying opportunity.

Two Timeframes - 2x Less Risk❗

You should always compare the chart in two different time frames to protect yourself from missing such moves.

Longer time frame to check the trend.

Shorter timeframe to find the best entry.

But what exactly is a time frame?

When selecting a time frame, you’re selecting how much detail you want to see. They offer a different perspective of the same thing 👇

We recommend using no more than 2-3 different time frames depending on your trading style.

Here’s a cheat sheet👇

Your chosen style may change as you get better. You might go from very active trading to a more hands off approach or vice versa, but there’s a small detail you should be aware of:

Shorter time-frames will make you better, faster!

When swing trading or investing, you are making only a few trades every month. Let’s say that every trade you close, whether it’s profit or loss, gains you XP points, just like in a game.

Both scalpers and day traders will open a few trades every day, thus at the end of a month the amount of XP points acquired will be a lot more for the short-term trader.

In a single day, a day trader may encounter crises, bull markets, crashes, and everything else in between.

Long-term traders may have to wait weeks or months to spot such patterns, and even longer to adjust to them. If you don’t have the time to be scan the charts all-day you should absolutely focus on longer time frames or copy trading.
