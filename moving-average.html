Moving Average

4 min read

You’ve learned the chart patterns that will generate 80% of your profits and the most important indicator to confirm them.

There is one more to help you form a very profitable trading system in all markets.

This indicator smooths out the noise by calculating a simple trendline based on the average price over time. It’s called “moving” because it’s continuously adjusted by the latest price data.

The MA can act as both a support and resistance level - a concept you are already familiar with. 👇

Do you notice the similarities MA has with line chart? 🤔

Both use an average value but the MA has one key advantage - it can be used in tandem with a candlestick chart.😲

This gives you the opportunity to do the “two-timeframe analysis” in a single chart️👇

In the example above you can see that the price of Ethereum is forming the “ascending triangle pattern” on the 15 minute chart. In addition, the price is breaking above it’s longer 1-hour MA.
Buyers are gaining momentum and the pattern has a high likelihood of breaking out higher.

The MA shows you who’s been in charge, buyers or sellers for a longer period of time, while allowing you to scan for more immediate details with shorter time frame candles at the same time!

Bonus Indicator: RSI

The Relative Strenght Index is used to differentiate between two different market conditions. When combined with a strong pattern it can be a reliable guide to buy or sell.

The RSI is shown as an oscillator - a line graph moving between two extremes 0 - 100.

-RSI above 70 means the asset is being overbought or valued above its fair value. In such scenarios, the price is expected to fall soon.

- RSI of 30 or below indicates an oversold or undervalued condition, this often attracts buyers who want a cheaper entry.
