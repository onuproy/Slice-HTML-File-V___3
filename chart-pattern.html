Support and Resistance

Identify support and resistance areas - where the price failed to break above or below a minimum of three times and reversed.

If the price bounces off either level again, open a sell position at resistance or a buy position at support.

Set your stop-loss a few pips above the resistance and below the support.

Tip From a Pro

Very often a resistance level becomes the new support after a price breakout, while the old support, if broken, becomes a new resistance level. These are called key market levels and they are perfect opportunities to enter trades

This is especially true for Psychological support and resistance levels which often appear at round numbers as buyers and sellers prefer to trade larger positions at prices like $10, $100, $150, $10k, etc.

Trend Momentum Strategy

The support and resistance concept also works when trading pullbacks in strong uptrends and downtrends.

These happen as early traders cash out their profits from the trend, leading to a temporary price correction.

Identify a strong trend, and draw a trendline connecting the pullback lows.

Buy as the price bounces off the trendline, place the stop-loss a few pips below.

Draw a second trendline connecting the highs. Now you can estimate when the next correction may occur and sell in advance.

Tip From a Pro

Longer trends are stronger than short trends but strong fundamental events can reverse or correct any trend. Ride them while you can and change your strategy when they end.

The Head and Shoulders

This pattern is a strong sign of an incoming reversal at the end of an uptrend. If the pattern appears inverted, it signals the end of a downtrend.

Identify the first shoulder - a price rise, followed by a peak, followed by a pullback.
